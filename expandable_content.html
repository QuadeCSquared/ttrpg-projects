Expandable and Collapsible Content

To create an abstracted mass combat system for use in the Pathfinder Second Edition (Remaster) ttrpg.

A settlement or nation can spend up to 20% of its annual income on Defense, and thus, on Battalions.
A nation uses its highest level settlement when calculating its income, with the assumption that other settlements that pay fealty to it are already contributing to its wealth.
An example army budget for a tier 2 or 3 settlement would be: 50% Infantry, 25% Archers, 20% Cavalry, 5% Utility.

Battalion

A collection of units that have special abilities and modifiers. Battalions battle with other Battalions for dominance.

Leader

Each Battalion has some leader that they look to for orders. For player battalions this is usually one of the players in charge of a battalion. For other battalions, the leader is usually some creature around the player's level acting as a combatant.

A player or creature can lead a number of units up to 25 * their level.
If the player or creature is level 9 or higher, increase the maximum number of units they can control to 50 * their level.
If the player or creature is level 16 or higher, increase the maximum number of units they can control to 100 * their level.

A Battalion can only have one Leader. A Leader can command multiple Battalions.

A battalion whose leader is dead or unconscious when the battalion takes its turn is Diminished (see Diminished).
